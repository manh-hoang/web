Crispy lotus root

Estimated price

$49

Resources

⭐Calories: 443 •Carbs: 75g •Fat: 12g •Protein: 9g

Lotus root

Cooking oil

Sauce (Japanese soy sauce, mirin, sugar, water)

Steps and time takes

1)The lotus bulbs are peeled and sliced ​​into thin slices about 3cm. Finished peeling, remember to put lotus roots in water to avoid bruising. + You should choose fresh lotus and when cut is still crispy, it will taste better. (5m)

2) Place the pan over medium heat and pour in oil. Wait until the cooking oil is about 180 degrees. (Or you can test it by dipping the chopsticks, seeing a large effervescent is OK). (3m)

3) Lotus root to drain the water, in turn drop into the pan of oil. (4m)

4) When the lotus is ripe, the two sides will tighten and start turning from ivory-white to brown, then take out. Depending on the thickness of the cut lotus and the intensity of the fire, it usually takes about 3-5 minutes. If you want to eat more crunchy, let it take a little longer. (About 10m)

5) Place a pan over medium heat, add 1 teaspoon of Japanese soy sauce: 1 teaspoon of mirin: 1 teaspoon of sugar: 2 teaspoons of water in a saucepan, bring to a boil, and then turn off the heat. This sauce I do is loose and quite pale. If you want to eat richer, you can remove the amount of water on the recipe. + Lotus root snack eaten with sauce will be better. (5m)
